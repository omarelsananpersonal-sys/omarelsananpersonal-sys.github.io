What Is a Peltier Cooling Unit?

A Peltier Cooling Unit is a thermoelectric device that pumps heat from a cold side to a hot side using the Peltier effect. They are used in lab cold plates, beverage coolers, and precision temperature control systems. Each unit consists of four components — an ASA polymer housing, a TEC module (bismuth telluride), a 12V blower fan, and an ESP32 controller board — assembled and then thermal-tested to verify they can reach a 5 °C setpoint.

OEE Metrics

Overall Equipment Effectiveness (OEE) is calculated every 5 seconds and streamed live to InfluxDB. It is composed of three factors:

A
Availability
Run time ÷ Scheduled time
P
Performance
Actual ÷ Theoretical output
Q
Quality
Good units ÷ Total units
OEE
A × P × Q
World-class: ≥ 85%

Defect Classification

The production line simulates three failure modes, each tracked separately:

  • Mechanical — Warped or dimensionally out-of-spec housing (detected at incoming QC)
  • Electrical — Failed continuity on fan, board, or TEC module (detected at incoming QC)
  • Thermal — Unit cannot reach 5 °C setpoint — thermal runaway (detected at Thermal QC station)

Downtime Tracking

The HMI provides three stop modes, each logged separately to InfluxDB:

  • Emergency Stop — Unplanned downtime (equipment fault, unexpected halt)
  • Planned Stop — Scheduled maintenance, shift changes
  • Engineering Stop — Trials, changeovers, test runs

CMMS — Computerised Maintenance Management

A Flask web application manages scheduled maintenance for all five production machines. When HMI detects OEE below 70%, it automatically creates an alert in the CMMS.

  • Housing 3D Printer — nozzle wear, ASA print calibration (every 90 days)
  • Incoming Component QC Jig — probe calibration (every 180 days)
  • Assembly Jig & Press — pneumatic press calibration, thermal paste dispenser check (annual)
  • Thermal Test Bench — thermocouple calibration (every 180 days)
  • Packaging & Label Station — seal bar wear (every 90 days)

Thermal Telemetry

A separate process (producer.py) simulates a real thermal sensor on the Thermal Test Bench — running a PID-controlled TEC at 1 Hz, streaming temperature, cooler output %, fault states, and random TEC trip events directly to InfluxDB. This data feeds the Thermal Telemetry panel in Grafana.

Grafana Dashboard

The live dashboard includes 8 panels:

  • OEE Gauge (Red / Orange / Yellow / Green thresholds)
  • OEE Component Trends (Availability, Performance, Quality over time)
  • OEE Historical Trend
  • Assembled vs Rejected units over time
  • Total Assembled (stat card)
  • Total Rejected (stat card)
  • Defect Breakdown by Type (Mechanical / Electrical / Thermal)
  • Downtime by Category (Planned / Unplanned / Engineering)
  • TEC Thermal Telemetry (temperature vs 5 °C setpoint)
